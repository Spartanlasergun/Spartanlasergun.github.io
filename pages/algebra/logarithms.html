Logarithms
Logarithms were invented in 1614 by scottish mathematician John Napier as a way of simplifying calculations involving exponents. Logarithms are a fundamental mathematical concept used to solve a variety of problems involving exponential growth or decay, such as in science, engineering, and finance. They are essentially the inverse operation of exponentiation.
What is a logarithm?
Logarithms are a unique algebraic concept. In working with exponents, it is only natural that from time to time, special algebraic manipulation of exponents will become necessary. Consider the function: \[a = 2^b\] This is an interesting problem. What is the exact mathematical relationship between the variables \(a\) and \(b\)? Can we define \(b\) explicitly in terms of \(a\). If I told you that \(a = 10\), could you solve for \(b\), i.e.: \[10 = 2^b\] Using an exhaustive numerical technique, we can perhaps arrive at an answer: \[2^2 = 4\] \[2^3 = 8\] \[2^{3.1} = 8.5742\] \[2^{3.2} = 9.1895\] \[2^{3.3} = 9.8491\] \[2^{3.31} = 9.9177\] \[...\] But of course, such a method is not very practical. Alternatively, we can graph the function easily enough and estimate corresponding values of \(a\) and \(b\) based on our graph.
