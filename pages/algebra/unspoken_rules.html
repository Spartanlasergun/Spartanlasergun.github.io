Unspoken Rules of Algebra
Math can be described in many ways. It is a collection of rules based around systems of logic that described the real world in the most empricial way possible. Sometimes, necessary formalities are overlooked as being irrelevant, but I have found that a great deal of the intuition and pragmatic thinking necessitate some conception of these basic and seemingly inconsequential ideas. In this section, I will explore these ideas as the unspoken rules of mathematics, for you will seldom (if ever) come across these concepts throughout the course of a typical education.
Expressing Constants & Variables
Consider the following function: \[f(x) = 3x^2 + 5x - 1\] If I were to ask you to find and expression for the function \(f(x - \delta x)\), you would probably understand that there are many such ways to express this function. However, I want to consider one idea that might be a notch higher than the others. Observe: \[f(x - \delta x) = 3(x - \delta x)^2 + 5(x - \delta x) - 1\] \[f(x - \delta x) = 3(x - \delta x)(x - \delta x) + 5(x - \delta x) - 1\] \[f(x - \delta x) = 3(x^2 - 2x\delta x + \delta x^2) + 5(x - \delta x) - 1\]
