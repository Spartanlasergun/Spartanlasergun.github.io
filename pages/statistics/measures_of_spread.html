Measures of Spread
In statistics, measures of spread provide valuable information about the variability or spread of data points within a dataset. While measures of central tendency, such as the mean or median, offer insights into the typical or central value of a dataset, measures of spread help to understand how the data points are distributed around the central value. Though there are countless measurements that can be made with respect to the spread across a given dataset, over time several key fundamental ideas have taken precedence and are commonly used to describe the spread of data. These key ideas are discussed in this section.
Range & Interquartile Range
Range: The range is the simplest measure of spread, representing the difference between the largest and smallest values in a dataset. It provides an overall sense of the spread but can be sensitive to outliers.
Interquartile Range (IQR): The IQR represents the range between the first quartile (25th percentile) and the third quartile (75th percentile) of a dataset. It is robust to outliers and provides insights into the middle 50% of the data, making it useful for describing skewed distributions.
Mean Absolute Deviation (MAD)
Mean Absolute Deviation (MAD): The MAD measures the average absolute difference between each data point and the mean. The mean absolute deviation is a precise calculation whereas the standard deviation accounts for potential errors. This difference is critical to understanding why the MAD is used much less frequently in real life situations compared to the standard deviation. Standard Deviation
Standard Deviation: The standard deviation is the square root of the variance. It is widely used due to its intuitive interpretation and measures the average amount by which data points deviate from the mean. It provides a more meaningful understanding of the spread, especially when the data follows a normal distribution.
Variance
Variance: Variance quantifies the average squared deviation of each data point from the mean. It takes into account the entire dataset and provides a measure of how much the values vary from the mean.
